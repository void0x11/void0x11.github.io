6 Quick Ways to Judge PCB Design Quality

AA
Ahmed Mohamed Amin
Junior Electronics Engineer

Whether you're reviewing a contractor's work, evaluating a product teardown, or learning PCB design yourself, these six checkpoints will help you identify serious design flaws quickly.

1. Incorrect PCB Traces (Routing Issues)

  • Avoid 90° sharp angles: Use 45° or curved bends. Sharp corners can cause acid traps during manufacturing and impedance discontinuities.
  • High-current traces: Must be sufficiently wide to handle the current without overheating.
  • Critical analog traces: Do NOT run parallel to digital or fast-switching traces to prevent noise coupling.

Note: If you see sharp 90° angles, it often means the designer didn't configure their tools correctly.

2. Incorrectly Placed Decoupling Capacitors

Critical Rule

Missing or poorly placed capacitors is a major red flag.

  • Every IC power pin needs at least one decoupling capacitor (usually 0.1 µF).
  • Capacitors MUST be placed as close as possible to the power pin.
  • If capacitors are placed far away, they become ineffective.

3. No Length Matching on High-Speed Signals

This is critical for clocks and parallel buses (like DDR memory). Traces must have equal length so signals arrive at the exact same time.

  • Look for serpentine traces (wiggles) used to add delay to shorter lines.
  • Signals in the same group should have the same number of vias.
  • Warning: If high-speed lines lack length matching, timing errors are likely.

4. Improper RF Feed Lines

Feed Lines

  • Must use controlled impedance (typically 50 Ω).
  • Do not add series resistors unless calculated.

PCB Antennas

  • Place on the edge of the board.
  • NO ground plane should exist under the antenna.
  • Keep clear of other components.

5. Component Placement

Inductors

Separate them to avoid magnetic coupling. If they must be close, orient them perpendicular (90°) to each other.

Heat Management

Keep hot components (regulators, power resistors) away from temperature-sensitive parts like crystals or sensors.

Switching Regulators

Group switching components tightly. Keep them isolated from sensitive analog circuits to reduce noise.

6. Poor Grounding

A solid ground plane is preferred. For 2-layer boards, use a star ground topology. Never daisy-chain grounds.

Poor grounding causes voltage drops and signal noise.

Summary Checklist

Item Status Notes
Trace Angles No 90° bends Use 45° or curves
Decoupling Close to Pins Every IC power pin
High-Speed Length Matched Check for serpentines
RF Lines 50 Ω Impedance Specific width required
Grounding Solid/Star No daisy-chains

Use this checklist to quickly spot major design errors on any PCB.
