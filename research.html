Decentralized Visual-Localization Framework for TurboPi Swarm

Leading development of a decentralized visual-localization framework for TurboPi swarm robots, enabling GPS-free relative positioning through multi-robot feature sharing and geometric triangulation.

Capstone Project

● Completed 2025

Low-Cost Dual-Band Radar System for Drone Detection and Tracking

Designed, implemented, and experimentally validated a bistatic 5.8 GHz SDR-based FMCW radar and a 24 GHz Doppler radar within a $1,000 budget. Demonstrated the feasibility of 3D localization through dual-receiver TDoA/AoA processing.

Key Contributions

  • Mitigated LO leakage and DAC artifacts using custom DC-block and Sallen–Key filters on 24 GHz K-LC7 frontend.
  • Empirically validated Doppler motion sensing reliability and identified FMCW ranging limitations for small-RCS drones.
